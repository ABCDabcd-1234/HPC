CELL 7 — TF-IDF VECTORIZATION

MOST IMPORTANT NLP CONCEPT.

vectorizer = TfidfVectorizer(max_features=5000)
WHAT IS TF-IDF?

TF-IDF =

Term Frequency - Inverse Document Frequency

Used to convert text into meaningful numerical vectors.

WHY NOT SIMPLE WORD COUNT?

Simple count has problems.

Example:

the movie was good

Words like:

the
was

appear everywhere.

They are not useful.

TF-IDF reduces importance of common words.

TWO MAIN PARTS
1. TF (Term Frequency)

Measures:

how often word appears in document

Formula:

TF(t)=
Total terms in document
Number of times term t appears in document
	​


2. IDF (Inverse Document Frequency)

Measures:

how unique/rare word is

Formula:

IDF(t)=log(
Documents containing term t
Total Documents
	​

)

FINAL TF-IDF SCORE

TF-IDF(t)=TF(t)×IDF(t)

EXAMPLE

Suppose:

Review:

"movie was amazing"

Vector becomes:

amazing	boring	movie
0.8	0.0	0.2
max_features=5000

Keeps only top 5000 important words.

Why?

Without limit:

vocabulary becomes huge
memory usage increases
training becomes slow
